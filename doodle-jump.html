Welcome to Doodle Jump: The Endless Jumping Adventure!

Join our cute and bouncy character on an exciting sky-high journey! Leap through platforms, dodge enemies, and collect power-ups to set new high scores.

Game Controls

Doodle Jump features easy-to-use controls that enhance the fun and excitement of the game. Here's a breakdown of the controls:

  • Tilt Controls: Tilt your device left and right to steer your character between platforms. Use precise tilting to avoid obstacles and collect power-ups.
  • Jumping: Tap anywhere on the screen to make your character jump. Timing your jumps is essential to reach higher platforms and avoid falling off the screen.
  • Shooting: If you have a shooting power-up, tap on enemies to shoot them down, clearing the path for higher jumps.
  • Power-ups: Collect power-ups scattered throughout the game to help boost your journey and achieve a higher score.

How to Play Doodle Jump

Embark on your vertical adventure with Doodle Jump by following these simple steps:

  1. Select your character to start your adventure.
  2. Use tilt controls to steer left and right as you hop between platforms.
  3. Tap anywhere to make your character jump. Time your jumps carefully to avoid falling off the screen.
  4. Collect power-ups and use shooting abilities to clear enemies out of your way.
  5. Aim to achieve the highest score by jumping as high as possible without falling or getting caught by enemies.

Tips and Tricks for Success

To master Doodle Jump and reach new heights, consider these strategies:

  • Platform Strategy: Plan your landings and jumps carefully to avoid falling or encountering dangerous platforms and enemies.
  • Collect Power-ups: Prioritize grabbing power-ups that can help clear your path and make your journey smoother.
  • Watch Out for Enemies: Stay alert and eliminate enemies as they approach, using your shooting power-ups to stay safe.
  • Use Tilt Wisely: Master the tilt controls to navigate through tight spaces and reach platforms that may seem just out of reach.
  • Bounce on Springs: Springs give you a major boost into the sky! Time your jumps carefully to get the most out of them.
  • Combo Combos: Build combo points by quickly landing on multiple platforms or shooting down enemies in succession. This boosts your score and unlocks achievements.
  • Stay Centered: Keeping your character in the center of the screen gives you a better view of upcoming platforms and enemies, making it easier to react quickly.
  • Upgrade Your Shooter: Collect shooter power-ups to increase your firepower and accuracy, helping you eliminate enemies more efficiently.

About Doodle Jump

Doodle Jump is a fun and addictive endless jumping game that was first released for the iPhone on March 15, 2009, by Lima Sky. With simple mechanics and a charming doodle-style aesthetic, the game has gained millions of fans worldwide.

As you leap from platform to platform, you’ll encounter various enemies and power-ups, all while aiming to reach the highest possible score. The hand-drawn graphics, catchy sound effects, and fun gameplay keep players coming back for more.

Game History and Developer

Doodle Jump was created by Lima Sky, founded by Igor and Marko Pušenjak in 2008. Since its release, Doodle Jump has become a mobile gaming phenomenon, available on various platforms, including iOS, Android, PlayStation, and even VR headsets like Google Cardboard. With over 1 billion downloads, it remains one of the most iconic mobile games of all time.

In addition to Doodle Jump, Lima Sky is known for other hit games like Jetpack Joyride, Color Switch, and Crossy Road. The company’s games are recognized for their addictive gameplay, colorful graphics, and engaging music.

Timeline of Doodle Jump's Success

Here’s a timeline of key events in the history of Doodle Jump:

  • 2008: Lima Sky founded by Igor and Marko Pušenjak.
  • 2009: Doodle Jump released for iPhone on March 15, and later for Android.
  • 2010-2014: Doodle Jump expands to other platforms, including BlackBerry, Windows Phone, PlayStation, and more.
